**Who this is for:** a seed-stage founder running founder-led sales who wants enterprise-grade routing and follow-up without hiring a RevOps team.

**By the end you can:** auto-route qualified accounts to the right owner, enroll them in the right sequence, alert that owner, and exit the sequence the moment a lead replies or converts — so nobody is ever still being sequenced after they've booked.

**Prerequisites:** finish [Track 1 — Capture and qualify](/learn/capture-and-qualify/) (or know how to filter and score), and skim [Triggers, explained](/explanation/triggers/).

## The mental model

Build the system before the message. Routing decides **who** owns an account and sequencing decides **what** they get — and the unglamorous half, clean enroll/exit hygiene, is what keeps the motion from embarrassing you. Every enroll needs a matching exit.

## Modules

1. **Get the right rep on it** — [Route inbound by territory and seniority](/guides/route-and-sequence/territory-routing/), then [Round-robin lead routing](/guides/route-and-sequence/round-robin-routing/) for even distribution with a guaranteed first touch.
2. **Make stage changes visible** — [Slack deal alerts on stage change](/guides/route-and-sequence/slack-deal-alerts/). Kill the "what happened to that deal?" archaeology.
3. **Enroll and exit cleanly** — [Enroll and auto-exit sequences](/guides/route-and-sequence/sequence-enrollment/). The paired build that stops double-touching.

## Challenge

Wire routing into enrollment: a qualified lead is round-robined to an owner, gets a first-touch task, is enrolled in the matching sequence, and — through a second, paired workflow — exits that sequence the instant they reply. Test the exit path on purpose; it's the half everyone forgets.
