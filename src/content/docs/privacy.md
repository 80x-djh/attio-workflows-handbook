This site is run by Dan Hull (80x), an independent consultant. It is a free documentation resource, not a data business. Here is what is collected and why.

**Last updated: June 11, 2026**

## What is collected

- **Analytics.** The site may use Google Analytics 4 with IP anonymization to understand aggregate usage — which pages are read, roughly where visitors come from, and what devices they use. Analytics may not be active yet; this policy covers it from the moment it is enabled. No analytics data is used to identify individual visitors.
- **Server and CDN logs.** The site is hosted on Vercel, which keeps standard server logs (IP address, user agent, requested URL, timestamp) for operating and securing the service. See Vercel's own privacy policy for retention details.

## Cookies

When Google Analytics 4 is active, it sets cookies or similar identifiers to distinguish visitors. There is no other tracking — no ad pixels, no fingerprinting, no third-party advertising scripts.

## Mailing list

If you join the mailing list, you give your email address voluntarily. It is used only to send handbook updates. Every email includes an unsubscribe link, and unsubscribing takes effect immediately. Your address is never sold or shared for marketing.

## Third parties

- **Google** — analytics processing, when GA4 is enabled.
- **Vercel** — hosting and CDN, including server logs.
- **GitHub** — if you open issues or pull requests on the handbook repository, GitHub's own terms and privacy policy apply to that activity.

## Your rights

If you are in the EU/EEA or UK, you have the right to access, correct, or delete personal data held about you, and to object to or restrict its processing. For any request, email [dan@80x.ai](mailto:dan@80x.ai) and it will be handled directly. You can also complain to your local data protection authority.

## What is not done

No personal data is sold. There are no ads. Nothing is collected beyond what is described above.

## Changes

If this policy changes, the update will be noted on this page with a new "last updated" date.
